PMA Company Establishment in Indonesia

A PMA (Penanaman Modal Asing) is a foreign-owned limited liability company in Indonesia, regulated under Indonesian Investment Law and supervised by BKPM through the OSS system.

PMA allows foreign investors to legally own shares, operate businesses, hire expatriates, open corporate bank accounts, and obtain investor visas or KITAS.

Main requirements include shareholders data, company structure, business classification (KBLI), minimum capital commitment, and registered business address.

Our team assists you from initial consultation, KBLI analysis, deed establishment, government approvals, until full operational readiness.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can foreigners own 100% of a company in Indonesia?
Yes. Foreign investors may own up to 100% of company shares, depending on the business sector in accordance with Indonesia’s Positive Investment List.
How long does it take to establish a PMA company?
The establishment process typically takes between 7–14 working days after all required documents are completed and approved.
Is a local director required for a PMA?
No. A PMA may be fully managed by foreign directors, subject to applicable immigration and work permit regulations.
What is KBLI and why is it important?
KBLI is Indonesia’s official business classification system that determines licensing requirements, ownership limitations, and regulatory obligations for each business activity.
